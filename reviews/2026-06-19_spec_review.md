## 1. Summary Verdict

**Score: MAJOR REWORK**

The three documents tell a coherent story (tiered free-stack migration with eval-based go/no-go) but are not execution-ready. They contain **at least 9 internal contradictions**, **5 spec gaps that prevent fixture files from loading**, and **2 critical mismatches with existing repo code** (wrong embodiment-id namespace, wrong `LLMRequest` interface). The provider-name risk surface is high: most model IDs in the eval-runner / shortlist are not in `LLMProvider` enum, three env-var hierarchies collide, and the rollback plan is incomplete because of hard-coded `grok-3` / `grok-3-mini` defaults in `src/clients/llmClient.xai.ts:67,75`. The eval suite will fail to load fixtures the day it runs. Treat the package as a **design intent** that needs ~3 days of fixing before coding starts.

## 10. Verdict Recap

**Overall: MAJOR REWORK.**

The migration spec, eval suite spec, and openrouter shortlist are an internally consistent **design intent** but not an execution-ready package. Of the 12 contradictions, 5 are blocking (wrong embodiment-id, wrong interface, missing `LLMProvider` enum, missing fixtures, missing adapters). Of the 18 risks, 3 are critical, 9 are high, 5 are medium, 2 are low. The fastest path to "minor-fix" is: (a) collapse the 9 new `LLMProvider` enum members into 1 `openrouter` transport + 1 free-form `LLM_MODEL` string, (b) translate English → German embodiment ids at the eval-runner boundary, (c) decide on a real privacy posture for the consult endpoint, (d) ship the LLM-as-Judge in Phase 1 instead of the regex heuristic. After that, the spec is a 2-day refactor, not a 3-day rewrite.
